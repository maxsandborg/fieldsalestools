Section 2 — Problems Discovered

HIGH Problem #1: All 20 /alternatives/ pages return 404

The sitemap declares 20 alternatives pages (e.g., /alternatives/spotio-alternatives) and the homepage footer links to them, but every single one returns 404. These are high-value programmatic SEO pages targeting "[tool] alternatives" keywords — one of the highest-intent search patterns in SaaS directories.

Impact: 20 dead URLs in sitemap being submitted to Google. Footer links on every page pointing to 404s. Missing an entire keyword cluster worth an estimated 2,000-5,000+ monthly searches ("SPOTIO alternatives", "SalesRabbit alternatives", etc.). Google is wasting crawl budget on dead URLs and potentially penalizing site quality signals.

HIGH Problem #2: /stacks index page returns 404

The /stacks hub page is linked in the main navigation but returns 404. Individual stack pages like /stacks/roofing work fine. This means users clicking "Stacks" in the nav hit a dead end.

Impact: Broken primary navigation link. The /stacks section has 17 high-quality pages with no hub to tie them together for topical authority. Google sees a broken nav link on every page, damaging crawl signals.

HIGH Problem #3: No /alternatives/ index page exists

Even if the individual alternatives pages are fixed, there is no /alternatives index page to serve as a hub. This section is completely orphaned from the site's IA.

HIGH Problem #4: No /best/ index page exists

The 8 "Best X for Y" guide pages have no index hub at /best. These are valuable money pages that are effectively hidden — only discoverable through deep cross-links or sitemap crawling.

MEDIUM Problem #5: Compare page only surfaces 6 of 60 comparisons

The /compare hub only displays 6 "Editor's Comparisons" despite having 60 comparison pages in the sitemap. The other 54 comparison pages are only accessible via the dynamic dropdown or from individual tool pages — making them essentially orphaned from the hub page's link equity perspective.

Impact: 54 comparison pages get minimal internal linking from the hub. Google has to discover them through deep crawling rather than clear hub → spoke architecture. Massive missed opportunity for topical authority signals.

MEDIUM Problem #6: Category filtering uses query strings, not dedicated URLs

Categories like "D2D & Canvassing," "CRM & Pipeline," etc. use /tools?category=CRM%20%26%20Pipeline query string URLs. These are client-side filtered (same HTML payload) and create no unique indexable pages.

Impact: Missing dedicated category landing pages that could rank for "field sales CRM software", "door to door canvassing tools", "route optimization software" — all high-value keywords. Query string URLs are typically not indexed by Google.

MEDIUM Problem #7: Stacks pages lack internal link distribution

The 17 stack pages are linked from the homepage sidebar and footer but not from the relevant industry or tool pages. A /stacks/roofing page is not linked from /industries/roofing, and vice versa — missing obvious topical linking opportunities.

LOW Problem #8: Shallow content on industry index page

The /industries index page is just 8 cards with ~20-word descriptions each. No introductory content, no SEO copy, no cross-links to related /best/ guides or /stacks/ recommendations.

LOW Problem #9: No blog/content hub

The site has zero editorial/blog content. No /blog, /guides, or /resources section. All content is tool-focused with no informational content to capture top-of-funnel traffic or build E-E-A-T signals.
